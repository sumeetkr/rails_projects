About the Research

The rapid development in mobile sensor technologies makes collecting real-time daily activity data over large population possible. This continuous availability of a vast amount of data rescales the landscape of human-centric applications and sensing.
The significant expansion of data population from carefully controlled lab experiments to mass-generated real life mobile sensor data overwhelm the conventional "one-size-fits-all" modeling methods for activity recognition. The user heterogeneity in terms of age, behavioral patterns, lifestyle and so on brings in the Population Diversity Problem that degrades the performance of tradition activity recognition frameworks.

To address these challenges, Carnegie Mellon Lifelogger presents an ongoing research for Personalized Activity Recognition for Context-aware Mobile Systems. By introducing personalized and adaptive behavior modeling method, the system aims to index, recognize ,cluster, retrieve, and infer high level semantic meanings of user behaviors in a semi-supervised manner.
